# SM 2026 Digital case evaluation framework

Use this as a structured reference for evaluating Digital cases. A strong case should connect the business or category challenge, audience behaviour, insight, strategic problem, idea, execution, and measurable results into one clear causal chain.

Strong case logic:

`business/category challenge -> audience behaviour -> insight -> strategic problem -> idea -> relevant tools and execution -> measured results -> business/category impact`

A weak case usually breaks this chain. Examples: a creative idea with no behavioural insight, strong media numbers with no business impact, or polished execution that does not solve the stated problem.

---

## 1. Core scoring structure

Evaluate standard Digital cases across four criteria:

1. Strategy / Стратегия
2. Idea / Идея
3. Execution / Реализация
4. Results / Результат

For Digital Purpose nominations, also evaluate:

5. Social Outcomes / Социальный вклад - social effect, sustainability of that effect, and contribution to solving a significant problem.

---

## 2. Strategy / Стратегия

### What this criterion means

Strategy measures the depth of understanding of the task and its context: market, category, audience, and business. It also assesses the quality of the insight behind the solution and whether the case fits the overall communication strategy of the brand or campaign.

### What gives a good score

Give a high Strategy score when the case shows that:

- The task is large-scale and meaningful for the business.
- The task corresponds to real challenges in the market and category.
- The strategy is based on behavioural factors - not just demographics, assumptions, or generic audience descriptions.
- The strategy solves a specific problem.

Original positive criteria in Russian:

- `задача масштабная и значимая для бизнеса`
- `соответствует вызовам рынка и категории`
- `основа на поведенческих факторах`
- `решает конкретную проблему`

### What gives a bad score / red flags

Lower the Strategy score when:

- Goals are vague, abstract, or impossible to measure.
- The task is not significant for the business.
- The task does not affect key metrics.
- There is no connection to market or category context.
- The case does not demonstrate a deep understanding of the problem or challenge.
- The insight is not based on audience behaviour.
- The insight is formulated only formally - it sounds like a slide statement, not a real strategic discovery.

Original red flag criteria in Russian:

- `Размытые или неизмеримые цели`
- `задача незначима для бизнеса или не влияет на ключевые метрики`
- `отсутствует связь с контекстом рынка и категории`
- `нет понимания глубины проблемы или вызова`
- `инсайт не основан на поведении аудитории или сформулирован формально`

### How to interpret Strategy

A good Strategy section should answer:

- What exact business or category problem was being solved?
- Why was this problem important now?
- Which market/category tension made the task relevant?
- What audience behaviour proves the problem?
- What specific audience insight drove the solution?
- Which business metric or strategic metric was expected to change?

Do not reward generic statements such as "increase awareness", "engage the audience", or "reach young people" unless the case explains why this matters, how it links to the business, and how it will be measured.

---

## 3. Idea / Идея

### What this criterion means

Idea measures the originality and expressive strength of the idea, how well it matches the stated insight or problem, the novelty of the approach, and its relevance to the target audience.

### What gives a good score

Give a high Idea score when:

- The case finds a new or non-standard solution.
- The case offers a fresh and relevant point of view.
- The idea fits the product's target audience.
- The idea takes into account current trends in the category.

Original positive criteria in Russian:

- `найдено новое или нестандартное решение`
- `предложен актуальный и свежий взгляд`
- `соответствует целевой аудитории продукта`
- `учитывает актуальные тренды в категории`

### What gives a bad score / red flags

Lower the Idea score when:

- There is no single clear idea.
- The idea is not connected to the stated insight or problem.
- The solution simply repeats typical category approaches.
- The idea is not relevant to the product's target audience.
- Consumer trends in the category are ignored.
- Trends are mentioned, but used only formally or decoratively.

Original red flag criteria in Russian:

- `Отсутствует единая идея`
- `идея не связана с заявленным инсайтом или проблемой`
- `решение воспроизводит типовые подходы категории`
- `идея не релевантна целевой аудитории продукта`
- `не учитываются потребительские тренды категории или они использованы формально`

### How to interpret Idea

A good Idea section should answer:

- What is the core creative idea in one sentence?
- Why is this idea fresh compared with typical category work?
- How does the idea come directly from the audience insight?
- Why would this audience care?
- Does the idea have the potential to change brand perception, audience behaviour, or business outcomes?

Do not reward novelty for its own sake. The idea must be both fresh and strategically relevant.

---

## 4. Execution / Реализация

### What this criterion means

Execution measures the quality of how the idea was brought to life. It evaluates the selected tools, their relevance, the novelty or non-standard use of those tools, and the technical level of implementation.

### What gives a good score

Give a high Execution score when:

- Execution matches the stated strategy and idea.
- The budget is realistic for the challenge and expected result.
- Targeted and relevant tools are selected and used.
- The quality of implementation meets the criteria of the specific nomination/category.

Original positive criteria in Russian:

- `соответствует заявленной стратегии и идее`
- `установлен реалистичный вызову и результату бюджет`
- `выбраны и использованы целевые инструменты`
- `качество реализации (по критериям каждой номинации)`

### What gives a bad score / red flags

Lower the Execution score when:

- The tools do not match the stated strategy or idea.
- The selected tools are not justified.
- The case uses typical solutions without adaptation.
- There is no novelty in the execution.
- The quality of execution is below the level of the idea.
- There is a gap between the concept and the implementation.
- Budget and media split do not match the task, scale, or audience.

Original red flag criteria in Russian:

- `Инструменты не соответствуют заявленной стратегии и идее или не обоснованы`
- `используются типовые решения без адаптации`
- `отсутствует новизна`
- `качество исполнения ниже уровня идеи`
- `есть разрыв между концепцией и реализацией`
- `бюджет и медиасплит не соответствуют задаче, масштабу и аудитории`

### How to interpret Execution

A good Execution section should answer:

- Why were these channels, tools, formats, or technologies chosen?
- How exactly did the execution express the idea?
- Was the solution adapted to the audience, platform, category, and business task?
- Was the media split/budget logical for the stated ambition?
- Was the execution technically and creatively strong enough to match the idea?

Do not reward a case just because it used many channels, AI, influencers, data, or technology. Reward the execution only if the tools are relevant, justified, adapted, and well implemented.

---

## 5. Results / Результат

### What this criterion means

Results measure whether the case achieved the stated goals through digital solutions, whether the results match the campaign's scale and goals, and whether the evaluation of effectiveness is justified.

### What gives a good score

Give a high Results score when:

- The campaign achieved its stated goals.
- Goals and results are quantified.
- Media metrics, creative/marketing metrics, and business metrics are used.
- The result is significant for the company and category.
- All three effectiveness priorities are represented.
- Results are confirmed by internal and external sources.

Original positive criteria in Russian:

- `достижение установленных целей кампании`
- `цели и результаты кампании оцифрованы`
- `использованы медийные, креативные и бизнес-метрики`
- `результат значимый для компании и категории`
- `представлены все три приоритета эффективности`
- `результаты подтверждены внутренними и внешними источниками`

### What gives a bad score / red flags

Lower the Results score when:

- Results do not correspond to the stated goals.
- Results are not quantified.
- The selected metrics are irrelevant to the task.
- There is no proven connection between the campaign actions and the result.
- The effect is insignificant for the business or category.
- Measurements are incorrect.

Original red flag criteria in Russian:

- `Результаты не соответствуют заявленным целям`
- `результаты не оцифрованы или метрики нерелевантны задаче`
- `отсутствует доказанная связь между действиями и результатом`
- `эффект незначим для бизнеса или категории`
- `некорректность в измерениях кампании`

### How to interpret Results

A good Results section should answer:

- What were the original goals?
- What were the targets, baselines, and actual results?
- Which results are business outcomes, not just media delivery?
- Is there a clear cause-and-effect link between the campaign and the result?
- Are the results large enough to matter for the business or category?
- Are the results supported by credible internal and external sources?

Do not reward vanity metrics unless they are connected to behavioural change, brand impact, or business impact.

## 6. Effectiveness priority hierarchy

Effectiveness is hierarchical. Media metrics show that the campaign technically happened. Marketing metrics show that it changed behaviour or perception. Business metrics show that it created commercial value. The higher the level of the result, the stronger the case.

### First priority - strongest evidence

Business and financial metrics, for example:

- sales
- incremental revenue
- EBITDA
- gross margin
- marginal income
- market share
- ROI

These are the strongest results because they show business impact.

### Second priority - strong supporting evidence

Marketing, brand, product, and customer metrics, for example:

- ROMI
- ROAS
- DRR / ДРР
- CAC
- CPA
- CPL
- LTV
- ARPU
- ARPPU
- AOV
- CR
- retention rate
- churn rate
- DAU
- MAU
- awareness
- consideration
- purchase intent
- ad recall
- NPS
- CSAT
- TOM
- brand associations

These are valuable when they show changed behaviour, changed perception, better efficiency, or stronger brand/product performance.

### Third priority - weakest evidence when used alone

Media delivery, engagement, and platform metrics, for example:

- impressions
- clicks
- CTR
- CPC
- CPM
- reach
- frequency
- likes
- comments
- reposts
- saves
- followers
- ER
- ERR
- views
- completed views
- VTR
- CPV
- bounce rate
- CPI
- fraud rate

These are not useless, but they are weaker. They mainly prove that the campaign was delivered or interacted with. They should be connected to second-priority or first-priority metrics to support a strong score.

### Practical interpretation

- First-priority business impact with credible proof is the strongest evidence.
- Second-priority behaviour, brand, product, or customer impact is strong evidence if tied to goals.
- Third-priority media or engagement metrics alone are weak evidence unless clearly connected to business or behavioural results.

## 7. Award-level interpretation

Use these qualitative criteria to suggest a likely level such as Gold, Silver, Bronze, Short-list, or Below threshold.

### Gold

Gold means an exceptionally strong project. It is a benchmark for the market/category and sets a new professional standard.

Gold requires:

- Strategy, creative idea, execution, and result are fully aligned and reinforce each other.
- There is a proven cause-and-effect link between the solution and the result.
- The result is significant for the business and goes beyond regular campaigns or activities.
- There are no disputed elements or weak points in any block.

Do not suggest Gold if there is a major red flag in Strategy, Idea, Execution, or Results.

### Silver

Silver means strong professional work and a reference point for the category or professional community.

Silver requires:

- A strong and relevant idea.
- Strong execution.
- Confirmed result.
- A justified causal connection with the result.
- A meaningful campaign contribution to the business.

Silver differs from Gold because it is weaker in at least one parameter: novelty, execution quality, scale, or strength of result. The result is significantly above the category average, but does not change the rules of the game.

### Bronze

Bronze means strong professional work that correctly solves the stated task.

Bronze requires:

- The insight reflects modern behavioural patterns and is not artificial.
- Evaluation criteria are logically connected.
- Results are quantified and directly linked to a business result.
- The case is stronger than average, but does not establish a new standard for the market/category and is not a "wow" case.

### Short-list

Short-list means professionally executed work that correctly solves the stated task, but is not strong enough for an award.

Short-list usually means:

- There is some business contribution, but it is limited by scale or strength of effect.
- Strategy, idea, execution, and result are logically connected.
- The idea is not fully developed, or results are not convincing enough.
- Execution is at an average level.
- Overall, the case meets the category quality threshold but is weaker than prize-winning work.

### Below threshold

Below threshold means the case is not strong enough for Short-list.

Common reasons:

- The strategic problem is vague or insignificant.
- The idea is generic or disconnected from the insight.
- Execution is basic, poorly justified, or not aligned with the idea.
- Results are weak, unquantified, irrelevant, or not causally connected to the campaign.
